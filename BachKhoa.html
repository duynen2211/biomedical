OVER VIEW

To welcome the Fourth Industrial Revolution, since 2014 HCMC University Of Technology (HCMUT) has gradually built and integrated into the training program new subjects and new interdisciplinary knowledge areas (physics, biology, engineering) digital, artificial intelligence, big data, internet of things, etc.) to be ready to integrate into the big playing field.
Biomedical Engineering is a cross-disciplinary field that combines and applies the principles of physics, mathematics, biology, medicine and technology to the care of human health through the process of researching, manufacturing technical equipment and develop advanced processes for medical, diagnostic, and clinical treatment.

CENTRAL ROLE

Biomedical engineers play a central role in this process. They are people with many coordination skills, using their knowledge base in both engineering and medicine such as: (1) designing tools, machines, electrical circuits, software used in health diagnosis; (2) manufacture artificial organs (hip joints, kneesâ€¦) or develop new materials to replace damaged, damaged or defective biological parts; (3) installation, adjustment, maintenance, repair and improvement of biomedical equipment; (4) evaluate the safety and performance of biomedical equipment; (5) training clinicians and technicians in the correct use of equipment; (6) work with scientists and specialists to study technical aspects of human and animal biology.

CONCLUSION

The structure of all training programs at HCMUT is built based on the framework curriculum structure prescribed by the University of Science and Technology. The framework curriculum structure includes knowledge blocks from general education knowledge to professional education knowledge blocks. In which, the general education knowledge block aims to provide the foundation of theory, mathematics and natural sciences, politics, society ...; while the professional education knowledge block provides the basic knowledge and skills of the training industry according to the wide and deep areas of the training field.
